SCIENCE

Science is the study of the natural world through observation and experiment. A scientific explanation uses observations and measurements to explain something we see in the natural world.

Science is derived from the latin word "Scientia" meaning "Knowledge". It's full meaning is:

  • S-systematic and
  • C-comprehensive
  • I-investigation and
  • E-exploitation of
  • N-natures
  • C-causes and
  • E-effects

    • Why Science courses are best for you to study.

    Beyond the potential scientific breakthroughs, there are individual benefits to learning science, such as developing our ability to ask questions, collect information, organize and test our ideas, solve problems, and apply what we learn.

    Scientific knowledge allows us to develop new technologies, solve practical problems, and make informed decisions: both individually and collectively. Because its products are so useful, the process of science is intertwined with those applications: New scientific knowledge may lead to new applications.

    List of science courses you might want to study

    Every course listed below has a potential to take you far in life. All that is required from you is to love the course, dedicate yourself to the course and be ready to give it your very best for you to excel in it.

    • Chemistrykeyboard_arrow_down
      Chemistry is the study of matter and the changes it undergoes and considers both macroscopic and microscopic information. Matter is anything that has mass and occupies space. The five main disciplines of chemistry are physical chemistry, organic chemistry, Inorganic chemistry, analytical chemistry and biochemistry.
      JAMB subject combination
      • Chemistry
      • Physics/Mathematics
      • Biology
      • English Language(compulsory)
    • Cell biology and Geneticskeyboard_arrow_down
      Genetics and cell biology is the science of heredity. It is studied at the level of whole organisms (classical or transmission genetics), the DNA itself (molecular genetics), or whole populations (population and evolutionary genetics).
      JAMB subject combination
      • Chemistry
      • Physics/Mathematics
      • Biology
      • English Language(compulsory)
    • Biotechnologykeyboard_arrow_down
      Biotechnology is a technology that involves the use of living organisms. Biotechnology is mainly used in agriculture, food science, and medicine. In biotechnology, living organisms are used to make useful chemicals and products or to perform an industrial task.
      JAMB subject combination
      • Chemistry
      • Physics/Mathematics/Futher Mathematics/Agricultural Science
      • Biology
      • English Language(compulsory)
    • Bioinformaticskeyboard_arrow_down
      Bioinformatics is the application of information technology to the study of living things, usually at the molecular level. Bioinformatics involves the use of computers to collect, organize and use biological information to answer questions in fields like evolutionary biology.
      JAMB requirements for this course
      • Mathematics/Physics
      • Biology
      • Chemistry
      • English Language
    • Cyber Security Sciencekeyboard_arrow_down
      Cyber security is the field that specializes in protecting networks, companies, and individuals from attacks. Cyber attacks can include unauthorized access, viruses, spyware, spam, botnets, phishing scams, and more. In other words, cyber security is an essential part of the broader field that is computer science.
      JAMB requirements for this course
      • Mathematics
      • Physics
      • Chemistry/Biology/Agric science/Economics/Geography
      • English Language(compulsory)
    • Earth Sciencekeyboard_arrow_down
      It is an integrated study of the Earth's history, composition, and structure, its atmosphere and oceans, and its environment in space. Knowledge of Earth Science is important because most human activities are related to interaction with the planet Earth.The four major branches of Earth science are geology, meteorology, oceanography, and astronomy.
      JAMB requirements for this course
      • Mathematics
      • Biology
      • Chemistry
      • English Language(compulsory)
    • Forensic Sciencekeyboard_arrow_down
      Criminal and forensic science majors will study both science and criminal justice. In this major, you will learn how to reconstruct crimes and analyze physical evidence such as blood, DNA, fingerprints, and other evidence and how to use it in a court of law.
      JAMB requirements for this course
      • Mathematics/Physics
      • Biology
      • Chemistry
      • English Language(compulsory)
    • Industrial Physicskeyboard_arrow_down
      Industrial Physics. Physicists in industry have made possible the technological advances of modern products that make our lives safer, better, and easier as well as improving the security and infrastructure of our Nation. More than 50% of physics students get their first permanent job in industry.
      JAMB requirements for this course
      • Mathematics
      • Physics
      • Chemistry/Biology
      • English Language(compulsory)
    • Industrial Chemistrykeyboard_arrow_down
      Chemistry in its industrial applications especially to processes in manufacturing and the arts and to commercial production of chemicals. Industrial chemists work in many different industries – including petrochemicals, polymers, plastics food, cosmetics, pharmaceuticals, minerals and new materials. You might also find an industrial chemist working in marketing or management in the chemical industry. Industrial chemistry is the link between the research and industrial-scale chemical engineering. In a general sense, industrial chemists are involved in: Safety and efficiency – industrial chemists are constantly striving to improve the safety and efficiency of making important chemicals and materials.
      JAMB requirements for this course
      • Mathematics
      • Biology/Physics/Agric Science
      • Chemistry
      • English Language
